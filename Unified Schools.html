What is Unified Schools

Special Olympics Unified Schools is an education-based project and youth-driven initiative. Through sports and education activities, we inspire young people to become advocates for positive change in their communities, helping people with intellectual disabilities gain respect and dignity. Unified Schools embodies acceptance and inclusion, promoting respect for differences and sharing friendship and joy. The program began its first year of activities in 2008.

Unified Schools Development in East Asia

2014 East Asia was officially included in the US-China Strategic Dialogue People-to-People Exchange
July 10, 2015 US-China joint announcement launching Unified Schools in China
Since 2015 Gradually launched across Mainland China and the five other Programs: SO Chinese Taipei, SO Hong Kong, SO Macau, SO Korea and SO Mongolia

Unified Youth Leadership

Special Olympics defines leadership as a relationship in which one person influences the behavior or actions of others to achieve goals.

Core Elements of Youth Leaders

  • Know: Understanding and recognition of yourself and your partner
  • Accept: Acceptance of differences and similarities
  • Utilize: Co-leadership, teamwork, communication, environment building
Six Leadership Skills
Communication Decision Making Adaptability Relationship Building Goal Oriented Continuous Improvement
