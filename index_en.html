What is Special Olympics?

Special Olympics is a non-profit organization providing year-round sports training and athletic competition for people with intellectual disabilities

🏆

Global Movement

Special Olympics has 200+ member organizations worldwide, hosting Summer and Winter World Games annually, with world-class competitions every two years.

❤️

Beyond Sports

Special Olympics focuses not only on athletic performance but also on athletes' physical and mental health. Health programs cover vision, hearing, dental, and more.

🤝

Inclusion

Through sports activities, people with and without intellectual disabilities participate together, learning inclusion and respect, building accessible communities.

🌟

Unleashing Potential

Enabling people with intellectual disabilities to unleash their potential in sports, express themselves bravely, share joy with family and friends, and live a wonderful life.

Origin Story

How a backyard summer camp changed the world

Founder: Eunice Kennedy Shriver

Eunice Kennedy Shriver was a member of the Kennedy family and sister of John F. Kennedy. For over 50 years, she dedicated herself to improving the quality of life for people with intellectual disabilities, changing the world's perception of this population through action.

Her motivation came from her sister Rosemary—Eunice witnessed firsthand the unfair treatment Rosemary endured due to her intellectual disability, which inspired her lifelong commitment to fighting for the rights of people with intellectual disabilities.

Eunice Kennedy Shriver

The Backyard Summer Camp

Special Olympics began with Eunice organizing summer camp activities in her backyard for people with intellectual disabilities. At that time, she invited all people with intellectual disabilities and their families from the surrounding community to participate in the summer camp in her backyard. Simple sports activities allowed everyone to feel the warmth of acceptance and inclusion for the first time.

Camp Shriver Activities

The Special Olympics World Games

On July 20, 1968, the first Special Olympics World Summer Games was held at Soldier Field in Chicago, USA. Hundreds of athletes with intellectual disabilities from the United States stepped onto the sports field for the first time. Since then, Special Olympics rapidly spread across the globe, becoming a social movement for equal opportunity for people with intellectual disabilities.

Unified Sports

People with and without intellectual disabilities form teams together to train and compete, breaking barriers through sports and helping everyone find a sense of belonging in sports

🏅 Competitive

Teams have similar numbers and skill levels, with athletes with and without intellectual disabilities competing together, demonstrating true sportsmanship.

📈 Developmental

More skilled athletes help less skilled members learn, achieving mutual growth through mentorship and cultivating team spirit.

🎉 Experiential

Focus on participation and experiencing the power of inclusion, allowing more people to experience the joy and connection that sports bring.

Unified Schools

The Special Olympics Unified Schools program has covered 28,000+ schools in 151 countries, with over 1 million youth participants.

In China, as of 2026, East Asia Region has over 1,500 Unified Schools joined, with over 500 Unified Schools in mainland China.

China and Special Olympics

Deep historical ties, glorious Special Olympics memories

2007 Shanghai Special Olympics World Summer Games

12th Special Olympics World Summer Games Shanghai 2007

In 2007, the 12th Special Olympics World Summer Games was held in Shanghai. This was an unprecedented event in Special Olympics history. More than 7,300 athletes from 165 countries participated, with 40,000 volunteers providing service.

President Xi Jinping, then Secretary of the Shanghai Municipal Party Committee, participated throughout the entire event. The World Games served as a preview of the 2008 Beijing Olympics, demonstrating China's support for inclusion to the world.
