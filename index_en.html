What is Special Olympics?

Special Olympics is dedicated to providing year-round sports training and competition opportunities for children and adults with intellectual disabilities, enabling them to develop their potential, demonstrate courage, and share joy, skills, and friendship with other athletes, families, and the community.

🏆

Global Movement

Special Olympics International is a non-profit organization that provides year-round sports training and competition for people with intellectual disabilities, with over 200 accredited Programs worldwide, hosting nearly 60,000 events annually, serving over 4 million Special Olympics athletes and Unified partners.

❤️

Beyond Sports

Special Olympics includes not only sports training and competition for people with intellectual disabilities, but also many non-sports programs: Healthy Athletes, Unified Schools, Young Athletes, Athlete Leadership, Family Engagement, and more.

🤝

Unified Philosophy

Through sports and non-sports programs, people with and without intellectual disabilities participate together, learning inclusion and respect, building accessible communities together.

🌟

Unlocking Potential

Enabling people with intellectual disabilities to realize their potential through sports, demonstrate courage, share joy with family and friends, and live a wonderful life.

Origin Story

How a summer camp that started in a backyard changed the world

Founder: Eunice Kennedy Shriver

Eunice Kennedy Shriver was a member of the American Kennedy family and sister of John F. Kennedy. For over 50 years, she dedicated herself to improving the quality of life for people with intellectual disabilities, changing the world's perception of people with intellectual disabilities through her actions.

Her motivation came from her sister Rosemary - Eunice witnessed the injustices her sister faced due to intellectual disability, which inspired her to dedicate her life to fighting for the rights of people with intellectual disabilities.

Eunice Kennedy Shriver (left) and her sister Rosemary

Camp Shriver

Special Olympics began with a summer camp that Eunice organized in her own backyard for people with intellectual disabilities. At that time, she invited people with intellectual disabilities and their families from surrounding communities to participate in summer camp activities in her backyard. Simple sports activities allowed everyone to feel the warmth of acceptance and inclusion for the first time.

Special Olympics World Games

On July 20, 1968, the first Special Olympics World Summer Games were held at Soldier Field in Chicago, USA. Hundreds of athletes with intellectual disabilities from across the United States stepped onto the sports field for the first time. Since then, the Special Olympics movement has spread rapidly around the world, becoming a social movement fighting for equal opportunities for people with intellectual disabilities.

Unified Sports

Bringing people with and without intellectual disabilities together to train and compete, breaking down barriers through sports, and helping everyone find belonging

🏅 Competitive

Equal numbers of players with similar skill levels. Athletes with and without intellectual disabilities compete together, demonstrating true sportsmanship.

📈 Developmental

Higher-skilled players help lower-skilled ones learn, achieving growth together through mentorship and developing teamwork spirit.

🎉 Recreational

Focus on participation and experiencing the joy of inclusion, letting more people feel the happiness and connection that sports bring.

Unified Schools

The Special Olympics Unified Schools program has reached 24,000+ schools in 151 countries, with over 1 million youth participating.

In China, as of 2026, the East Asia region has over 1,500 schools participating, including over 500 Unified Schools in mainland China.

Healthy Athletes

The Special Olympics Healthy Athletes program aims to provide free health screenings through a pleasant, friendly environment, eliminating the anxiety and fear that people with intellectual disabilities often experience when facing doctors. The program helps develop athlete health, enabling most athletes to perform at their best in competition and training, showcasing their talents and skills.

Currently covers 9 disciplines: Special Smiles, Opening Eyes, Health Promotion, Strong Minds, FUNfitness, Healthy Hearing, Fit Feet, MedFest, and Young Athletes Health

Young Athletes

Special Olympics Young Athletes is a sports and play program for children ages 0 to 7 with and without intellectual disabilities. Young Athletes learn basic sports skills like running, kicking, and throwing. The program creates opportunities for families, teachers, caregivers, and community members to share the joy of sports with children. Both children with and without intellectual disabilities can participate and benefit from it.

China and Special Olympics

Deep historical ties, glorious Special Olympics memories

2007 Shanghai Special Olympics World Summer Games

12th Special Olympics World Summer Games Shanghai 2007

In 2007, the 12th Special Olympics World Summer Games were held in Shanghai, an unprecedented event in Special Olympics history. Over 7,300 athletes from 165 countries participated, with 40,000 volunteers providing services.

Xi Jinping, then Party Secretary of Shanghai, was fully involved in this event. This Special Olympics World Games served as a preview for the 2008 Beijing Olympics, demonstrating China's support for the philosophy of inclusion to the world.
